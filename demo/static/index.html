Client learns the cryptographic suite + public parameters to blind in-browser.
What this means
Your browser gets the public key needed to encrypt your secret before sending it to the server.
Why it's safe
The server only shares public information. Your secret never leaves your device unencrypted.
2) Prove eligibility without sharing your secret
eligible-only
Add demo-code in "Issuance code" field, then click "One-click" to reveal this step.
What this means
You prove you have a valid issuance code without revealing the code itself to the server.
Why it's safe
Blind encryption means the server signs your request without ever seeing your actual secret or code.
3) Redeem and get a disposable token
one-time
Redeem once to receive a one-time capability token (e.g., submission token).
What this means
You exchange your signed credential for a one-use token that proves you're eligible without revealing who you are.
Why it's safe
The token is disposable and unlinkable. The server only stores a "nullifier" to prevent reuse, not your identity.

Privacy posture (demo)

PAAP helps remove identity from the authorization path. Operational logging still matters.

You store
Hashed issuance-code record, spent nullifiers, short-lived capability tokens.
You don’t store
Raw issuance codes, identity→credential mapping, identity attached to submissions.
Still possible
Timing correlation by network observers. For “extreme” anonymity, use a relay/Tor + avoid body logging.
